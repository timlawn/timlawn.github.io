About

I am a postdoctoral research fellow at Harvard Medical School and MGH working within the Jacobs (Blue Spot) and Sepulcre labs. My present research focuses on imaging of brainstem nuclei using highly multi-modal and graph theoretic approaches to map the molecular and systems level mechanisms underlying resilience and heterogeneity in ageing.

I am deeply interested in all aspects of conscious perception and cognition, especially when investigated through the lens of pharmacological or pathophysiological disruption. Two of the most fundamental roles of science are to help us make sense of ourselves within the universe and help those trying to survive within it. I feel neuroscience is currently uniquely placed to do so given that consciousness remains one of the final frontiers of human understanding, and neuropsychiatric diseases remain amongst the most debilitating intractable conditions of the 21st century. Thus, in my research I endeavour to shed light on neural mechanisms underlying both health and disease to provide both conceptual and translational value.

When I'm not doing neuroscience, thinking about neuroscience, or rambling about neuroscience at the pub, you can probably find me climbing, tinkering with my motorbike, or off on some backpacking adventure.
